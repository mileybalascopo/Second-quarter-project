Being a Pisay scholar is not easy. It involves numerous challenges and obstacles. The rigorous academic demands, standards, and curriculum can be overwhelming. It isn't always sunshines and rainbows and that's why support is needed sometimes ! Here are some tips on how to survive your stay in Pisay.

How to survive Pisay?

  • Manage your time wisely

    • Create a schedule and plan out your study and leisure time. It is important to manage your academic and leisure time wisely.

  • Be organized

    • Make a to-do list and a planner to keep track of your pending assessments. Collect and keep your checked assessments in an folder or an organizer.

  • Use multiple learning resources
  • Advance study lessons

    • It is better to be prepared than to be confused. Advance studying can help you grasp the concept better and be a step ahead of others.

  • Take notes during class

    • This will help you understand the topic better and will help you in your studies.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help

    • Group studies & review sessions with your peers are important to get feedback regarding your performance on the subject

  • Try doing the Feynman method when studying

    • The Feynman method is a method for learning and understanding complex topics more deeply. You simply pretend you are teaching the concept to someone else and try to identify the gaps in your understanding. By doing this method, you are reinforcing your existing knowledge of the subject, actively enhancing your comprehension and retention of the concept.

  • Try making flashcards and reviewers.

    • This is very helpful when studying for examinations and long quizzes. This will help you remember the lessons better and understand the concepts more.

  • Take breaks

    • Practice the 3R, Read, Review, Relax. Prioritize your mental health. Having a good mental, physical, and emotional health can help you achieve your goals. Please do not sacrifice your health in return of your desired scores and grades. Always remember that your well-being is your utmost priority.

  • Don’t hate the subject that you find hard !!!

    • Hating the subject will only make you lose interest in improving in the subject. Instead, use your frustration as an inspiration to do better on the subject.

  • embrace challenges as opportunities for growth

    • mistakes are room from improvement. Don't let your mistakes or failures define who you are. Use them as an opportunity to grow and improve your skills. Never give up.

  • celebrate your achievements no matter how small they are

    • Be contented of your achievements and don't compare your skills to others. Everyone is special and has their own strengths and weaknesses. It is important that you focus on your own growth rather than competing with others.
